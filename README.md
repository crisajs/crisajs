I'm a software engineer based in São Paulo. Most of my work starts the same way: someone has a real problem, and I figure out how to solve it with software — then I build it, ship it, and keep it running.

Lately that has meant a lot of WhatsApp automation, AI integrations and education platforms. I coordinate the **Instituto Saber & Cuidar**, where I'm responsible for everything tech-related, and on the side I run projects for restaurants, gyms and small businesses that need software that just works.

I like clean architecture, good docs and small teams that ship.

<h3>Instituto Saber & Cuidar</h3>

</div>

Brazil needs far more trained caregivers than it has, and most people who want to do this work can't afford a course. The Instituto Saber & Cuidar exists to close that gap: free, accessible training for elderly caregivers and healthcare workers, in São Paulo.

I coordinate the institute and built its technology from scratch — the same WhatsApp-first approach I use in my products, applied where it matters most. No app to download, no laptop required. If you have WhatsApp, you can learn a profession.

Of everything I work on, this is the project I'm proudest of.
